LOG.1 - THE SYSTEM

The TRAPPIST-1 system is located at RA 23h 6m 29s; Dec -5° 2′ 29″, within the constellation Aquarius. It is 40.66 light years away from Earth. Within the system there are 7 planets, labeled TRAPPIST-1b/h, all of which orbit around TRAPPIST-1a.

The passage of time is localized via 24 hour cycles. This ensures that the human sleep cycle is kept intact. Surrounding each planet is a mothership, labeled Vexis-1/7, the functioning capital of each planet until each planet is successfully colonized.

LOG.2 - Project Astrexus

Date: 2104
Head of Project: Siderus Corp.
Head Engineer: Elliot Vexis

GOAL: Find and Colonize habitable planets to preserve human kind.

LOG.2.1 - Construction

Led by head engineer Vexis, construction has been a smooth process. 7 freighter ships are to be constructed, allowing for one to orbit each planet in the TRAPPIST-1 system.

Ship Frame: All ships are created with a Titanium frame; lightweight and durable. Each unit is fabricated with a LRU - Large Rotational Unit. This allows for the simulation of gravity on each ship. The cockpit, engine bay, and artillery units are not included within the LRU.

Cockpit: The cockpit of each unit is equipped with the following: Guidance System, powered by the Uraxis AI; the main display console, a direct communications device, and all devices needed to control the internal safety of the Vexis units.

The first batch of ships will be named after the head engineer.

LOG.2.3 - Fuel

Each Vexis unit is powered by The Core. They are massive nuclear reactors that power the ships. Located within the engine bay, they are not affected by the artificial gravity of the LRU.

It is rated to run smoothly with a ship population around 1.36 billion on the biggest ships. Exceeding this limit can cause the power grid to malfunction. In this event, the Uraxis AI is programmed to reroute the energy to ship function necessities.

LOG.2.4 - Life within Vexis

The Vexis units are built with the intent to home millions of people. With this in mind, this is where certain ships differ from each other. Every ship is self sustainable to a degree, but they are built to benefit from each other.

Vexis Fleet Specifications

Vexis-1: Created to orbit around TRAPPIST-1b, this ship is intended for engineers and electrical workers. The ship is equipped to keep these workers in good condition. It is not luxurious by any means, but good enough to live there for an extended period of time.

Vexis-2: Created to orbit around TRAPPIST-1c, this ship is the smallest of the project. TRAPPIST-1c shows promise of useful minerals underneath its surface. This ship is equipped to support a working class, with not many amenities. However, due to its smaller size, it is one of the quicker and more maneuverable ships.

Vexis-3, 4, & 5: Created to orbit around TRAPPIST-1d, e, and f respectively, these are the biggest ships of the fleet. They are designed to mimic modern cities on Earth. Prioritizing human comfort, each ship has a side designation.

Specialized Vexis Units

Vexis-3: Specializes in manufactured goods, with a decent sized research department. Its docking station is dedicated to trade, and it is a central point to get resources from one ship to another.

Vexis-4: Specializes in research and scientific innovation. It has a smaller docking station, but still sizable enough to fit many ships.

Vexis-5: Specializes in agriculture. Its docking station is intended for bigger transport ships, as to get food and other resources to the other ships.

Vexis-6: The harshest ship out of all. At the time of liftoff, it is currently only inhabited by law enforcement. This ship will be used to house prisoners of the other ships as the journey to TRAPPIST-1 commences. When all the ships settle into orbit, Vexis-6 is set to orbit around TRAPPIST-1g. This planet will be used to hold prisoners, and the ship is set to be the head of judicial operations.

Vexis-7: Created to orbit around TRAPPIST-1h, this ship was created with one word in mind: Luxury. The ship was to house the higher ups of society, and mainly those working at Siderus Corporation. Not much else is known about the internals of the ship.

▼ RUNWAY'S EXCLUSIVE: A LEGEND'S RETURN

THE RETURN OF THE CHAMPION: SYPHERON VALTROS

Interview by Alai Rae | Photos by NO.5

When Sypheron disappeared, the racing world lost more than a champion. They lost a myth. The record-breaker. The showstopper. The one who hit 1,200 km/h and made it look like art. No warning, no explanation, just a sudden silence from one of the fastest racers the tracks had ever known.

Three years and five months later, Valtros is back, piloting a ship that could turn the gods green with envy.

We caught up with the elusive champion in a rare, sit-down interview that spanned laughs, confessions, and a few cigarette breaks.

RUNWAY: Sypheron Valtros. Champion. Ghost. Legend. You vanished. No press, no farewell tour. Where the hell did you go?

SYPHERON: Damn. Not even a "hi, how are you?" But fair. I dipped. Hard. Took some time off to deal with... family stuff. Third-grade cousin was sick—real bad. Needed someone. And hey, I'm many things, but I'm not gonna leave a kid behind.

RUNWAY: Three years and five months. That's a long time for "family stuff."

SYPHERON: Time moves weird when you're not the one holding the stopwatch. Besides, some of it was recovery time. I had a pretty nasty accident during training. Freak storm, whole cliffside gave way. I woke up with half my limbs not where I left them. Then an Aethercat decided I looked like lunch. Took the rest. ...Recovery was hell. But hell builds character, eh?

RUNWAY: Holy shit. You're lucky to be alive.

SYPHERON: I know, right? One minute I'm going 1,200 km/h, next thing I know I'm trying to out-crawl a massive beast with wings. Honestly, I screamed into a void and came out fabulous. What more do you want?

RUNWAY: And now you're back. With VANTABLACK. A ship whose name's been whispered like it's a legend. You own it now?

SYPHERON: Damn right I do. Blacker than blackout, faster than your last breakup. He's built for 1,500 km/h but I think we can crack higher. Top speed clocked at 1,507 km/h on my last test. Ain't he pretty?

RUNWAY: Give us the specs. The people are dying to know.

SYPHERON: Alright so...on top of my head... The engine is a Dual Pulse-Core HZ thrusters with ether-linked override. The exterior is a triple-coated anti-sensor mesh that can also absorb light. The custom HUD is my design. He hates losing~

RUNWAY: You've always been... different from the rest of the racers. Secretive. Flashy but private. What keeps you going?

SYPHERON: Spite. I like proving people wrong. That's it. Someone says "you can't," and I say "watch me." That mindset kept me alive, kept me fast, and sure as hell kept me dangerous.

RUNWAY: Rumors say you used to mod other ships back in the day. Some even whisper you're why Z-9 was "haunted" that year.

SYPHERON: Wrong one. People just love spooky stories. I'm just a humble racer. There's one of us that really loves to tamper with ships though. For legal reasons, cannot say who.

RUNWAY: Any hard feelings toward racers who took the spotlight while you were gone?

SYPHERON: …You mean LaBarge? Not at all. She just held my seat. Now I'm back to take it.

RUNWAY: Speaking of—you've also been spotted glaring daggers at Velázquez. History?

SYPHERON: That walking lawsuit? No comment. He knows what he did.

RUNWAY: And your biggest rival? Still LaBarge?

SYPHERON: Always has been. Always will be. Every time we're on the track together, somebody cries, somebody crashes, and the rest of the galaxy thanks us for the show~ She's fire. I'm acid. Sparks are guaranteed!

RUNWAY: You've always been private. No bodyguards. No public team. And no entourage. Why?

SYPHERON: Because I like my peace, and I like my power. The more hands on your wheel, the easier it is to crash. I trust my own grip.

RUNWAY: What's next for Valtros? More races? Fame? Trouble?

SYPHERON: Yes. Okay, seriously. I'm here to shatter expectations. To make old legends look like warm-up acts. The break was long. The comeback's going to be violent.

RUNWAY: Final question. You've crashed. You've lost limbs. You've survived it all and a damn Aethercat. What scares you now?

SYPHERON: Quitting. Giving up. Just… That's the only thing that's ever scared me. And I'm not about to start.

RUNWAY: Sypheron, you maniac. Welcome back.

SYPHERON: Pleasure's mine.

Runway would like to thank Sypheron Valtros for their time, and advises all readers not to try racing, survival, or taming Aethercats at home. Unless you hate your limbs.
